The Story of Alem

"Before Project HOPE, when my period started, I stayed home. Now, I have my pads, and I don't miss school anymore." - Alem, 14

For millions of girls in Ethiopia, the onset of menstruation is a sudden end to their education. Lacking affordable sanitary products, girls resort to using rags, leaves, or cow dung, leading to infections and, inevitably, shame-driven absenteeism.

A typical girl misses up to 50 days of school per year, putting her at a massive disadvantage. Project HOPE intervenes at this critical juncture by providing reusable pads that last for years, breaking the cycle of period poverty and ensuring girls stay in school.

Our solution is simple, local, and sustainable. We manufacture the pads in Ethiopia, creating employment for women while delivering dignity and education to girls like Alem.

The Impact of a Kit

"We aren't just giving pads; we are investing in a girl's future, her health, and her ability to complete her education."

The impact of a single reusable kit extends far beyond hygiene. It is a catalyst for economic independence and generational change.

  • **Prevents School Dropout:** Keeps girls in school during menstruation, directly boosting graduation rates.
  • **Saves Families Money:** Eliminates the monthly financial burden of disposable products for up to five years.
  • **Promotes Health:** Reduces the risk of infection caused by using unhygienic materials like rags and leaves.

Our commitment is long-term. By funding local production, we embed the solution within the community, ensuring sustainability long after our initial distribution phase.
