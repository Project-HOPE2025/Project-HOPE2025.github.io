The Problem: Period Poverty

In Ethiopia, a lack of menstrual products means that an estimated 1 in 3 schoolgirls miss up to 50 days of school per year, eventually leading many to drop out. This loss of education perpetuates the cycle of poverty.

1 in 3

Schoolgirls miss class due to menstruation.

50 days

The total school days missed annually by affected students.

15%

The proportion of girls who drop out entirely due to period poverty.

Alem's Story: The Human Impact

"I stayed home and worried. I knew I was falling behind. The dream of becoming a nurse felt further away with every passing day."

Alem, 14, from a rural community outside Addis Ababa, faced this impossible choice. When her period started, she lacked the means to manage it hygienically. She would often miss school, using old rags that were uncomfortable, insecure, and unsanitary.

The shame and fear of staining her clothes in class became overwhelming. She started to miss critical exams and fell behind her classmates. She contemplated quitting school, believing her education was over.

With your support, Project HOPE provides girls like Alem with a sustainable solution that restores their dignity and their future.

Beyond Pads: The Ripple Effect

"The power of a reusable pad kit is not the cotton and thread; it's the 3-5 years of uninterrupted education it provides."

By preventing dropout, your donation does more than provide a sanitary product—it breaks the cycle of poverty. An educated girl is more likely to:

  • Marry later and have fewer, healthier children.
  • Earn a higher income, spending 90% of it back on her family.
  • Become a leader and advocate for her community.

This is a long-term investment in dignity, health, and economic empowerment for an entire community.
